## Overview

**Repair Below From Above** is a VR puzzle game created for **Epic Games MegaJam 2022** with the theme **"As Above So Below."** The core concept is asymmetrical spatial reasoning: the player must complete puzzles inside a closed box, guided only by information displayed on a monitor positioned above their workspace.

This was my first VR project in **Unreal Engine 5**, and it required rapid learning across the entire UE5 VR pipeline — from Blueprint scripting to lighting, animation, and optimization for VR performance.

## Core Concept

The player sits in front of a closed box. A monitor above provides visual cues about what needs to be assembled inside. The challenge is not just solving the puzzle — it's translating what you *see above* into precise physical actions *below*, where you can only reach but cannot see the full picture.

This asymmetrical design creates a natural cognitive friction: the player has to hold information in mind while manipulating objects they can't fully observe. It's a small but compelling example of how VR can create puzzle experiences that flat screens cannot replicate.

## My Role

I was responsible for the full scope of development on this project:

- **Concept and game design** — translating the jam theme into a playable VR concept
- **Full-stack VR implementation in UE5** — Blueprint scripting, scene assembly, interaction logic
- **Audio and visual effects** — creating feedback systems that help the player understand their actions inside the box
- **Animation and interaction tuning** — ensuring the VR interactions felt natural and responsive
- **Testing and optimization** — maintaining stable VR performance within the jam timeline

## What I Learned

This project was an intense compression of the full VR development cycle into a GameJam timeframe. It forced me to make quick decisions about scope, prioritize interaction clarity over visual polish, and deliver a complete playable experience in a very short window.

It also demonstrated that **asymmetrical information design** — where the player has access to different information in different modes — is a rich design space for VR puzzles, and one I'd like to explore further in future projects.
