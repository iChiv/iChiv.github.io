## Overview

**Findy Hunter** is an XR project focused on interactive object-finding mechanics. For me, this project was primarily a technical design challenge: how to integrate art, sound, interaction logic, and custom input into a coherent XR experience while working outside my usual engine environment.

## My Role

With my background in game design and production, I took on the **engineer role** on this project. Since most of my prior experience was in Unity and Unreal Engine, this project required adapting to a new toolchain while maintaining the same quality bar for interaction feel and system behavior.

My specific responsibilities included:

- **Resource integration and scene assembly** — placing art and sound assets into the editor environment and ensuring they function together
- **Behavior scripting** — implementing dialog logic, branching options, animation triggers, and sound effect playback
- **Custom gesture recognition** — developing and tuning gesture-based input for XR interactions
- **Cross-functional coordination** — bridging the gap between art direction and technical implementation to keep the project moving

## Key Challenge

The main difficulty was not in any single technical problem, but in **making an XR interaction feel responsive and natural while working with limited iteration cycles.** Custom gesture recognition in particular required repeated tuning: too sensitive and the system triggered accidentally; too strict and interactions felt unresponsive. Finding that balance was a matter of testing, adjusting, and testing again.

## What I Learned

This project expanded my engineering range and reinforced the importance of **integration thinking** in XR development. An XR experience is only as strong as the weakest link between its art, interaction, audio, and logic layers — and someone on the team needs to see the whole chain, not just their piece.
