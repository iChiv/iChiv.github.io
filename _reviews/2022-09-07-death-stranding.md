## Introduction

Death Stranding is a third-person game created by **Kojima Productions**, which producer Hideo Kojima calls a "Social bonding game."

The player plays as Sam, a courier belonging to the Bridge. After the outbreak of Death Stranding, the surface is no longer suitable for human survival. The player takes on this role, traveling west from the eastern United States, reconnecting the entire country to the Chiral network.

The game creates a unique connected experience by limiting player resource caps and asynchronous online play, giving players the spiritual satisfaction of finding themselves **"being needed"**.

![Gameplay](/assets/images/reviews/death-stranding-2.png)

## Creating Small Impulses on Interest Curve

In most games, player emotion is directly related to the tension of the storyline and combat difficulty. In Death Stranding, the asynchronous online system creates a unique connection experience, where players feel more pleasure from other players than the game itself.

![Analysis](/assets/images/reviews/death-stranding-3.png)

## Game Flow Analysis

- **Chapters 1-2**: The game teaches unfamiliar concepts, players have few resources, struggle with course management, and experience a depressing atmosphere
- **Chapter 3+**: Players receive carriers and exoskeletons, gain more control over delivery routes, and receive appreciation from other players
- **Highway Building**: Lack of supplies becomes apparent, incorrect planning creates dilemmas
- **Snowy Mountains**: Unable to use vehicles, returning to earlier gameplay, but zip lines provide new solutions
- **End Game**: Continuous boss battles provide new dimensions

![Environment](/assets/images/reviews/death-stranding-4.jpg)

## Innovative Mechanisms

### Resource Management from A to B

The game creates meaningful logistics challenges through weight management, terrain navigation, and equipment selection.

### Asynchronous Multiplayer

The appreciation system creates subtle emotional fluctuations throughout the experience curve, making players feel connected without direct interaction.
